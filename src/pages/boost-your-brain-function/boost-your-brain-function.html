Dementia

One of the major conditions that affect the brain is dementia which is also referred to as diabetes of the brain. The brain needs energy so as to be able to function. The main source of energy is glucose and ketones. Dementia is caused by the death of the brain cells which occurs over time. The death of brain cells is attributed to lack of enough energy to the brain to regenerate new brain cell. Therefore, the patient will continue to have decreased brain cells until the condition is noticed.

According to statistics, over 5 million Americans of all ages suffer from dementia. Although several factors are known to contribute to the disease, poor dietary habits have been linked to being a major contributor to the disease.
